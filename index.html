Wood Technology

Welcome To Wood Technology Page

The primary material involved in woodwork is timber usually obtained from trees.

Tree can be classified as:

  1. Exogenous: Those which grow outward from the center adding almost concentric layers of fresh wood from time to time. Wood obtained form exogenous trees is used extensively in engineering works.Exogenous trees can be further divided into:
    • Conifers or Evergreen
    • Deadous or Broad Leaf
  2. Endogenous: Those which grow inward or endwaeds. They are light and though but are too flexible to be used for engineering purposes e.g bamboos, palms and canes.

Types of Wood

Woods can be classified as:

  • Softwood: This is obtained from tress that have needle shaped surfaces or conifers e.g pine, walnuts, semal and sprue.They are widely used for construction.
  • Hardwood: Hardwoods are obtained form tress having broad leaves or deadous e.g sal, teak, mahogamy, shisloan, oak, ash, bamboo, neem, mango. It is widely used for doors, furniture, joinery

Characteristics

Timber in wood work should have the following characteristics:

  • It should be from te heart of tree and tree from sap.
  • It should have uniform dark color with close fibres.
  • It should be easily workable
  • It should be strong and heavy
  • It should have high resistance to fire

Seasoning Preservation of Timber

Seasoning of timber is the process of drying timber or removing moisture or sap present in a freshly felled tree under controlled conditions. The common methods are:

  • Natural Seasonig or Air Seasoning: In this method, tree is converted into logs. Space is ensured so that there will be free air circulation all around each pieces.
  • Artificial Seasoning or Kiln Seasoning: This is a very fast system that keeps the moisture content under control. Some of this chemical preservatives are tar oils, water soluble chemical and organic solvent chemical.

Woodwork Tools and Auxillary Material

Woodwork tools can be classified as:

  • Marking, Measuring and Testing Tools: e.g penil, compass, trammel points, sprint level. etc.
  • Cutting Tool: saw, chisel, axe. etc.
  • Planning Tool: plough plane, spokeshave, block plane, etc
  • Biring and Drilling Tools: Bradawal, Auger, Gimlet, etc
  • Fixing or Striking tool: dar hammer, mallet, etc
  • Wood Working Machines: Wood lathe, circular saw, band saw, jig saw, etc.
